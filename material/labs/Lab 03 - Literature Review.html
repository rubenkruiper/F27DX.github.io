# Lab - Literature Review Lab exercises to help you master the subject material and to complement the lecture material for Introduction to Interaction Design (F27DX). # Overview Your goal for this lab is to find three relevant research articles to your coursework 2 project. Possible domains of research include Interaction Design (IxD), Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and User eXperience (UX). * These articles will support your ideas and help you state problems you're trying to solve - which is important! Hence, the choice of keywords is critical. * Again you will not find articles which simply state the same idea If you need to, review the lecture slides on what makes a credible article. * Wikipedia and generally most online websites are not academic, while * Academic conference papers and journal articles (usually peer reviewed) are reliable sources. * Other possible criteria include: - Authority: what are the authors credentials? What type of site? Is the content peer reviewed? Who else cites the work? How many times? - Accuracy: are the facts verifiable? References included? - Objectivity: is it fact, opinion or propaganda? - (Out)datedness: Are the findings recent/up-to-date? Is the date indicated? # How can I find/retrieve literature? * This lab focuses on Google Scholar [link]. - Keywords are important, consider using advanced scholar once you know which keywords are relevant - Google Scholar directly tells you how many times the work has been cited elsewhere - Google Scholar provide automatically generated examples of formated references, these are not always correct/complete!
