# Lab - Problem Space Lab exercises help you master the subject material and complement the lecture material for Introduction to Interaction Design (F27ID). # Activities Planned for Lab 2 * You will come up with your project ideas: - Work individually on an IxD project, throughout the lab sessions - Choose a problem space - Plenary discussions of the research problem/design statement you will work on * Tips: Look at current solutions! Try to identify problems, or perhaps think of new functionalities/products. * Answer all questions briefly in your workbook! # Exercise 1 -- Problem Finding * Choose a problem space, describe as much as you know * Look for the inefficiencies with an interface or specific interactions * Who is having this problem? When do they have this problem? Why is it a problem? When does it become a problem? # Exercise 2 -- Defining your problem (in the real/current situation) * Real - What is the current state of the system, how does it operate in reality today? * Ideal - What could the desired system be? What are the Desirable products and activities - What is the possible solution, or the characteristics of a solution? # Exercise 2A. Real v Ideal: A step towards formulating your project idea * Describe a real-life situation where there are flaws/problems and where an interface/mobile app can help. * Consider alternative, existing solutions (even if not ideal). * Describe the real and ideal situation/scenario/context. # Exercise 3: Design Statement * From your chosen project idea, move towards finalizing its design statement * Design statement is a one line sentence that describes your project (and answers the who, what, where, why etc) * Our goal is to design a “____ (what)” for “____ (who)” that provides a “___ (why)” and enhances “___(why)” while in a “____ (where)” * Discuss your design statement with your tutor and clearly outline in your document (google drive, word doc) * What is your idea? Use Bill Verplank's framework to sketch out a conceptual model.
